Research

Technical vision

ORION develops the algorithmic and systems foundations for distributed, uncertainty-aware decision-making at the edge. The research spans multiscale inference, cooperative reasoning agents, and real-time optimization under resource and communication constraints.

Core question

How do distributed agents make good decisions in real time when models are uncertain and resources are limited?

Modern operational systems generate observations across many spatial and temporal scales, yet decisions must often be made locally, quickly, and with incomplete information. ORION addresses this by coordinating networks of reasoning agents that assimilate heterogeneous data, propagate calibrated uncertainty, and produce actionable inference on edge hardware.

01

Multiscale inference and assimilation

Fusing observations, surrogate models, and high-fidelity simulations across scales into coherent probabilistic state estimates that remain useful as operating conditions shift.

02

Cooperative reasoning agents

Designing agent networks that exchange beliefs, reconcile local context, and coordinate inference and control across distributed platforms with constrained communication.

03

Real-time edge optimization

Placing uncertainty-aware inference and decision logic on edge devices where latency, compute, and bandwidth constraints make centralized processing infeasible.

04

Robust decision-making under uncertainty

Developing control and optimization methods that account for model uncertainty, distributional shifts, and incomplete observability in safety-critical and time-sensitive settings.
